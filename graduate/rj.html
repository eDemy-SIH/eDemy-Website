    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you like chatting and have a lot of energy? Then you should think about being a radio jockey. A radio jockey is an individual who provides entertainment to radio listeners. They broadcast music, deliver news, interact with listeners, run competitions, and interview guests. On commercial music television stations, a video jockey is an announcer who presents and plays videos.

    The only difference between a radio and a video jockey is that a radio jockey does not appear on the screen, while a video jockey does. The field has seen some major changes, since its inception. The main job of an RJ and a VJ is to communicate with the audience. A prospective candidate should have excellent communication skills. They introduce music videos on Television and also give some background information about the music being played. They bridge the gap between the audience and the musician.

    Description

    Radio jockeys are in high demand in the private sector since the opening of the airwaves. They will collaborate with a variety of top FM stations as well as private radio projects. Video jockey is becoming an exciting career option for the music-obsessed generation, thanks to the availability of various music channels on television. The VJs' main task is to present the majority of the host music-related shows and music videos on television. Producers of music shows, music networks, and film-based programs are the most common employers of video jockeys. The need for these practitioners is growing all the time.

    Skills You Need

    • Vocal Skills: This does not mean that the candidate should have a loud harsh voice instead, it means that they should have a soothing and impactful voice.
    • Creative Skills: RJs and VJs sometimes have to be spontaneous. Their articulation should be creative enough to impress the listener.
    • Communication Skills: The main job of an RJ and a VJ is to communicate with the audience. A prospective candidate should have excellent communication skills.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science or Commerce or Humanities.
    • Graduation: Bachelors in Journalism / Mass Communication /Bachelor of Arts Honours. (English/Mass Communication) /Diploma or Certification in Radio/Video.
    • Post Graduation: Diploma in Radio Jockey.
