    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Are you intrigued by the various phenomena in the world of physics? If yes, then you should pursue a career in it. Physics is natural science and probably one of the oldest academic disciplines. It focuses on the study of matter, its motion and behaviour through space and time, and the related entities of energy and force. Physics is one of the most basic scientific disciplines, with the aim of understanding how the world works.

    It is concerned with the nature of matter and the relations between the observable universe's fundamental constituents. Physics advances frequently pave the way for new technologies. Advances in electromagnetism, solid-state physics, and nuclear physics, for example, led directly to the development of new products that have dramatically transformed modern-day society, such as television, computers, domestic appliances, and nuclear weapons.

    Description

    The physicist has to deal with all aspects related to matter and energy. A physicist studies the effects of forces, systems, and processes on the natural world. This job entails conducting extensive research in order to generate reports and technical papers that benefit the industry as a whole, as well as lecturing and educating students about physics laws. As a part of the analysis and evaluation of data, physicists perform complex calculations using computers. Their studies include the motion and properties of physical objects both large and small, the properties of waves, the properties of states of matter and the fundamental properties of matter and energy.

    Skills You Need

    • Technical Skills: Being a physicist is an academic field thus, the clear concepts of science and the passion for science is required.
    • Problem Solving Skills: Candidates should be able to identify complex problems and review related information to develop and evaluate options and implement solutions.
    • Thinking Skills: Professionals need to use logic and reasoning to find solutions and to reach conclusions.
    • Reading Skills: Professionals have to deal with a lot of documents so regular reading becomes an efficient skill.
    • Mathematics Skills: Physicists perform complex calculations which require clear concepts and interest in mathematics.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Science Honours in Physics or Applied Physics.
    • Post Graduation: Masters of Science in Physics
