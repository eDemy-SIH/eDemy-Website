    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you love aircraft and working with machines? If so, then a career as an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer is just for you. Aircraft maintenance is the execution of tasks required to ensure an aircraft or aircraft part's continued airworthiness, such as overhaul, inspection, replacement, defect rectification, and the embodiment of modifications, compliance with airworthiness directives, and repair.

    This is a very crucial profession as such professionals are responsible for maintenance, control and repairing of specialised aircraft parts and instrumentations. They are responsible for assessing the safety of the aircraft and repairing any inconsistency so as to prevent any malfunctioning. Assessing and handling repairs, executing regular inspections, and updating aircraft as needed are all part of this work.

    Description

    A licensed person who performs and certifies aircraft maintenance is known as an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer. The primary job of such professionals is to detect any detriment present in parts of the aircraft and repair or replace such flawed parts. They follow the engineering designs and plans and service the aircraft. They are also responsible for testing various gauges and instruments for any errors and work to solve any complaints registered by the pilots or co-pilots during the travel of the flight. It is a relatively challenging but rewarding profession. They are responsible for ensuring an aircraft operates properly and safely.

    Skills You Need

    • Mathematical Skills: This is a very technical profession, making such skills very important.
    • Concentration Skills: In this profession, missing even the slightest details can have disastrous consequences, making such skills very important.
    • Time Management Skills: You have to service an aircraft in between flights and thus have strict deadlines where delays may spell huge losses.
    • Pressure Handling Skills: The pressure to perform immaculate jobs in strict times makes pressure-handling skills very important.
    • Learning Skills: Even after studying, one has to keep up with the latest innovations in the field so as to ensure that all aircraft are fitted with up-to-date technology.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Technology in Aeronautical Engineering / Aircraft Maintenance Engineering License / Bachelor of Science.
    • Post Graduation: Aircraft Maintenance Engineer Training Courses.
