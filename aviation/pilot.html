    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you dream of taking control of the skies? If the answer is yes, then becoming a pilot might be your perfect career choice. A pilot is a skilled aviator responsible for operating aircraft, including airplanes and helicopters.

    Their role goes beyond simply flying the aircraft; pilots are entrusted with the safety and navigation of the entire flight. They must undergo extensive training and possess in-depth knowledge of aviation regulations, weather conditions, and flight systems. Pilots not only ensure a smooth takeoff, flight, and landing but also make critical decisions during emergencies, ensuring the safety and well-being of everyone on board.

    Description

    The role of a pilot is essential in ensuring the safe and efficient operation of an aircraft. Their responsibilities extend well before the plane takes off and continue until it safely lands. Their day typically starts with a comprehensive briefing, covering aspects such as weather conditions, flight duration, potential challenges, and more. Pilots are primarily responsible for the safe navigation and control of the aircraft, ensuring adherence to all aviation regulations and safety protocols. They collaborate closely with the flight crew, air traffic controllers, and ground staff to ensure a smooth flight. The working hours of a pilot can vary significantly, depending on factors such as flight routes, layovers, and the type of aircraft they operate.

    Skills You Need

    • Technical Proficiency: A pilot's primary duty is to safely operate the aircraft. They must have in-depth knowledge of aviation systems, navigation, and aeronautical principles, along with the ability to effectively use various flight instruments.
    • Problem-Solving Skills: Pilots are trained to handle unexpected situations and emergencies that may arise during flights. They must be able to think critically and make timely decisions to ensure the safety of the aircraft and its passengers.
    • Situational Awareness: It's crucial for pilots to have a keen sense of their surroundings, be aware of air traffic, and understand weather conditions. This helps them navigate and respond appropriately to changing circumstances during the flight.
    • Communication Proficiency: Pilots must communicate clearly and effectively with air traffic controllers, co-pilots, flight attendants, and other crew members. This ensures smooth coordination and information exchange throughout the flight.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor's Degree in Aviation Science / Diploma in Commercial Pilot Training (CPL).
    • Post Graduation: Commercial Pilot License (CPL) with Multi-Engine and Instrument Ratings.

    Pros

    • Being a pilot offers a unique chance to command an aircraft and travel to various destinations.
    • The profession doesn't necessarily require a specific degree, opening up opportunities for passionate individuals.
    • Pilots generally enjoy competitive salaries that tend to rise annually, along with perks like free flights and discounted hotel accommodations for themselves and their families.

    Cons

    • Seniority plays a crucial role in decision-making, be it in promotions or recommendations, which can sometimes be challenging for those lower in seniority.
    • The aviation industry is highly competitive, with companies often receiving an overwhelming number of applications, making it a tough field to break into.
    • Pilots often face an irregular and unpredictable work schedule, which may require them to work on holidays and weekends, impacting their work-life balance.
