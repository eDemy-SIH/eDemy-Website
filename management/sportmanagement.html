    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you love sports and want to be the ones managing them? Then a career in Sports Management just for you. Sports management is a multidisciplinary field that incorporates marketing, legal, finance, and business concepts. Sports management consists of being involved in the administration and working of sporting entities such as leagues, teams or sporting organisations. It is a highly rewarding profession for those who love sports and want to be a part of the events coming as successes.

    The front office system in professional sports, college sports managers, recreational sport managers, sports marketing, event management, facilities management, sports economics, sports finance, and sports information are only a few examples of sport managers. As one is involved in the management of entities, they need to be able to communicate well enough with others that they manage or negotiate with.

    Description

    A career in Sports Management includes working with the business aspect of the sporting industry. Those working in this field are responsible for overseeing the business and promotion of the entity that they manage. They monitor finances, negotiate upon contracts with the athletes and/or teams and also manage events that may fall into their jurisdiction. This is a booming profession and the opportunities in it are bound to increase. People who want to combine their passion for sports with business skills and pursue a successful career in this industry should pursue a Sports Management degree.

    Skills You Need

    • Communication Skills: As one is involved in the management of entities, they need to be able to communicate well enough with others that they manage or negotiate with.
    • Organisation Skills: At the end of the day, you are in the management of the industry and are primarily responsible for everything functioning efficiently, making organisational skills very important.
    • Time Management Skills: In the business of this industry, time can play a very important role in deciding the financial environment, making time management very important.
    • Analytical Skills: Since one needs to monitor finances, they need to have good analytical skills.
    • Decision Making Skills: One needs to be able to make decisions correctly and under pressure to work well-enough in this field.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science or Commerce or Arts (Humanities).
    • Graduation: BA. in Sports Management / BBA in Sports Management
    • Post Graduation: MBA in Sports Management/PG Diploma in Sports Management

    Pros

    • With sports growing financially more and more, this field has a very bright scope.
    • It allows you to work independently and with all the creativity that you want to.
    • It also allows you to work with big athletes and brands.

    Cons

    • It is not a job of fixed working hours and you may rarely get leaves.
    • You will have to be ever-present and at sporting events and manage the whole events.
    • It is a highly competitive field.
