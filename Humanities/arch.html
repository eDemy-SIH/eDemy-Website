    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Have you ever wondered about the ancient eras, and want to study them? Then Archaeology is the right choice for you! Archaeology is the study of the human past, human civilisations and living conditions from remains. A person who studies archaeology for a living is known as an archaeologist. The study of human activity via the recovery and examination of material culture is known as archaeology. Although archaeology is frequently thought of as a branch of socio-cultural anthropology, archaeologists also use biological, geological, and environmental systems in their research. Archaeology is especially essential for understanding about prehistoric societies because there are no written records for them. From the Paleolithic through the introduction of literacy in communities around the world, prehistory encompasses almost 99 percent of human history.

    Description

    An archaeologist mainly works with evidence found. In other words, they try and excavate material remains that can be evidence for human existence, civilizations, history, etc. These archaeologists then try to study their origin, describe the remains, and try to give it more context and build on to give us knowledge about an entire time period or era to pass on information to the world about their findings, they also publish their research. They also research the habits, values, and social patterns of many cultures. In their job, many archaeologists employ advanced instruments and technologies.

    Skills You Need

    • Research Skills: Research skills are like the foundation for archaeologists. For research they also need to have a good command of their writing skills to publish their findings.
    • Analytical Skills This is important because Archaeologists usually read and analyse data. They also need to apply the knowledge they have to make appropriate inferences.
    • Reading Skills: This is required to be able to comprehend written sentences and paragraphs, in work-related materials
    • Communication Skills It’s highly important because they'll be working with different people and surroundings. And they need to be able to effectively communicate their ideas and suggestions.
